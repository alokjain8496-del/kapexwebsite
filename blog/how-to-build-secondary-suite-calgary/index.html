How to Build a Secondary Suite in Calgary: Zoning, Permits & Incentives (2026 Guide)

A clear guide to property eligibility, permits, incentive funding, costs, and timelines for legal secondary suites in Calgary.

March 20, 2026 Kapex Infra Team Calgary, Alberta
Secondary SuitesBasement SuitesCalgary ZoningPermitsRental Income

Secondary suites are one of the most practical ways for Calgary homeowners to create rental income, increase property value, and make better use of existing space. With the City of Calgary offering up to $10,000 in incentives for qualifying projects, building a legal secondary suite has become more accessible than ever.

However, before starting construction, it is important to understand zoning permissions, permit requirements, and how the approval process works in Calgary.

👉 Learn more about requirements from the City of Calgary secondary suite guidelines.

Key takeaways

  • Secondary suites provide strong rental income potential.
  • Zoning and property eligibility must be confirmed first.
  • Permits and safety requirements are mandatory.
  • Incentives can offset costs but do not cover full construction.
  • Legal registration is required for compliance.

What is a secondary suite?

A secondary suite is a self-contained living unit within a home, typically located in a basement. It includes:

  • Separate kitchen.
  • Bathroom.
  • Sleeping area.
  • Private entrance.

According to the City of Calgary, all secondary suites must meet safety and design standards, including proper exits, ventilation, and fire separation.

Why Calgary homeowners are building secondary suites

Secondary suites are increasingly popular because they:

  • Create steady rental income.
  • Increase property value.
  • Support multi-generational living.
  • Use existing space efficiently.

They are also part of Calgary’s broader strategy to increase housing supply and affordability across the city.

What zoning rules matter most?

Zoning determines whether you can legally build a secondary suite on your property.

  • Most residential areas now allow secondary suites.
  • Common districts include R-CG and similar residential zones.
  • Some properties may still have restrictions based on local area plans.

Even though zoning has become more flexible, it is important to confirm your specific property details before starting design.

What permits are required?

To build a legal secondary suite in Calgary, you typically need:

  • Building Permit (mandatory).
  • Development Permit (if required).
  • Trade Permits (electrical, plumbing, HVAC).

A building permit is required before applying for incentives, and all work must meet current safety codes.

How does the Secondary Suite Incentive Program work?

The City of Calgary offers financial support through the Secondary Suite Incentive Program to encourage safe and legal housing.

  • Up to $10,000 per home.
  • Applies to suites within the main dwelling.
  • Must have an active building permit before applying.
  • Funding covers safety upgrades, not full construction.

👉 View official program details: Secondary Suite Incentive Program.

The incentive primarily covers safety improvements such as egress windows, smoke alarms, and fire separation systems.

What timeline should you expect?

  • Planning & design: 2–4 weeks.
  • Permits: 2–6 weeks.
  • Construction: 6–12 weeks.

Total timeline: 2–4 months.

Common questions homeowners ask

Is a secondary suite legal in Calgary?

Yes, secondary suites are legal in many residential zones, but they must meet zoning and safety requirements.

Do I need a separate entrance?

Yes, each suite must have its own accessible entrance, although shared stairwells are allowed.

Can I build a suite without permits?

No, permits are required to ensure safety and compliance with building codes.

What is the biggest mistake homeowners make?

Starting construction without confirming zoning or permit requirements.
