How to Build a Secondary Suite in Calgary: Zoning, Permits & Incentives (2026 Guide)

March 20, 2026 Kapex Infra Team Calgary, Alberta
Secondary SuitesBasement SuitesCalgary ZoningPermitsRental Income

Secondary suites remain one of the most practical ways for Calgary homeowners to create rental income, increase property value, and make better use of existing square footage. With the City of Calgary offering up to $10,000 in incentives for qualifying projects, bringing an existing basement or lower-level space into compliance can be more financially accessible.

Before construction begins, homeowners should first confirm zoning, understand permit requirements, and map out the approvals needed to deliver a legal suite that meets current life-safety and building-code standards.

Key takeaways

  • Secondary suites can create recurring rental income and long-term value.
  • Zoning and property-specific constraints should be checked before design.
  • Building, development, and trade permits may all be required.
  • Incentive funding can offset code and safety upgrades.
  • Layout, fire separation, and egress decisions all affect budget and schedule.

What is a secondary suite?

A secondary suite is a self-contained dwelling unit located within the main home, most commonly in the basement. It typically includes its own kitchen, bathroom, sleeping area, and a separate or dedicated entrance arrangement.

Why Calgary homeowners are building secondary suites

For many homeowners, a legal suite is attractive because it can support mortgage affordability, create flexible space for family members, and improve long-term resale appeal.

👉 Secondary suites also align with Calgary’s broader housing strategy to improve supply and affordability.

What zoning rules matter most?

  • Whether the land use district allows a secondary suite.
  • Any overlays, local policy, or parcel-specific restrictions.
  • Parking, access, and entry configuration expectations.
  • Window sizes, ceiling heights, and emergency egress conditions.
  • Existing utility and mechanical capacity inside the home.

What permits are usually required?

  • Building Permit.
  • Development Permit, when required by the site or design.
  • Trade Permits for electrical, plumbing, and HVAC work.

How should homeowners think about incentives?

The City of Calgary Secondary Suite Incentive Program is intended to help owners legalize and improve suites by offsetting qualifying safety and code-related work rather than full construction costs.

  • Funding can reach up to $10,000 per home.
  • An active building permit is generally required before applying.
  • Eligible work often includes safety upgrades and compliance improvements.
  • Program rules, funding windows, and documentation requirements should be reviewed early.

Common questions homeowners ask

Is a secondary suite legal in Calgary?

Yes. Secondary suites are permitted in many residential areas, but the property still needs to satisfy current zoning rules and the suite must be completed with the proper permits and inspections.

Do I need a separate entrance?

Most legal suites need a safe and dedicated access arrangement. The exact design depends on the existing home layout and applicable code requirements.

Can I build a suite without permits?

No. Permits and inspections are a core part of making the suite legal, safe, and eligible for relevant incentives.

What mistakes should homeowners avoid?

  • Starting work before confirming zoning and permit requirements.
  • Underestimating code upgrade costs for fire separation and egress.
  • Skipping feasibility review of mechanical and utility systems.
  • Assuming incentive funding will cover full construction costs.
